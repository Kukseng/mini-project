The Majestic Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is the largest religious monument in the world and the crowning jewel of Cambodia's ancient Khmer Empire. Built in the early 12th century, this architectural masterpiece spans over 400 acres and stands as a testament to the ingenious craftsmanship of the Khmer civilization.

Originally constructed as a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Vishnu for the Khmer Empire, it gradually transformed into a Buddhist temple by the end of the 12th century. The temple's design represents Mount Meru, the mythical home of the gods in Hindu mythology, with its five towers symbolizing the five peaks of the mountain.

The temple is admired for its grandeur and harmony of design, as well as the extensive bas-reliefs and devatas (guardian spirits) adorning its walls. The best time to visit is during sunrise or sunset when the temple is bathed in golden light, creating a truly magical experience that will stay with you forever.

Historical Timeline

Tracing the rich history of Angkor Wat and the Khmer Empire

802 CE

King Jayavarman II establishes the Khmer Empire and declares himself a "universal monarch" (chakravartin).

1113-1150 CE

Reign of King Suryavarman II, who commissions the construction of Angkor Wat as his state temple and eventual mausoleum.

1177 CE

Angkor is attacked and sacked by the Chams (from present-day Vietnam), a traditional enemy of the Khmer.

1181-1219 CE

Reign of Jayavarman VII, who builds Angkor Thom, Bayon, and numerous other temples and structures in the Angkor complex.

13th-14th Century

Gradual transformation of Angkor Wat from a Hindu temple to a Buddhist temple as Buddhism becomes the dominant religion in the kingdom.

15th Century

Angkor is gradually abandoned as the Khmer capital shifts to Phnom Penh. Angkor Wat, however, remains an important Buddhist center.

1860s

Rediscovery of Angkor by French naturalist Henri Mouhot, bringing the site to Western attention.

1992

Angkor Archaeological Park is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
